The most popular national park in California closed itself off in March
2020 when the Covid-19 pandemic hit. Yosemite National Park would go on
to remain in a state of restricted access through June 2020 until it
opened back up step-by-step in months to safely welcome its enthusiastic
annual visitors.

As a result of the national shutdowns, the United States National Parks
Service took
[measures](https://www.nps.gov/orgs/1207/statmentonparkopscovid19.htm)
to restrict access to the parks. Recommendations of washing hands and
social distancing already were being made while people were still
allowed to visit the parks. However, at the same time, the Secretary of
the Interior announced that
[fees](https://www.nps.gov/orgs/1207/national-park-service-to-temporarily-suspend-park-entrance-fees.htm)
were being waived for those visiting the parks with the suggestion that
those who are elderly and immunocompromised be careful.

At Yosemite National Park, the story was different. The park
indefinitely ended access including around the clock enforcement of the
closing. In accordance with national health guidelines, officials at
Yosemite also advised social distancing and hand-washing.

"The health and safety of our visitors, employees, volunteers, and
partners at Yosemite National Park is our number one priority," stated
the park's March 2020 [press
release.](https://www.nps.gov/yose/learn/news/yosemite-national-park-is-modifying-operations-to-implement-latest-health-guidance-2.htm)

It wasn't until later that June that Yosemite National Park introduced
the day pass reservation system. The system was in place to limit the
number of guests to the park as one could only visit if they had a day
pass made through Yosemite's online reservation system. The reservation
system was used
[through](https://www.nps.gov/yose/learn/news/yosemite-national-park-continues-a-phased-reopening-on-thursday-june-11-2020.htm)
November of 2020 after which the park was opened up again. However, the
system was
[reimplemented](https://www.nps.gov/yose/learn/news/yosemite-national-park-to-re-implement-a-day-use-reservation-system-beginning-on-friday-may-21-2021.htm)
in the summer in 2021 when some of the largest periods of park
visitation take place.

Visitor numbers to the national parks dipped in 2020 with the various
restrictions and closures implemented by the different State and
National Parks Departments, along with general Covid-19 quarantining.
Visitation for the year dropped by 25.7% from 2019 to 2020, according to
[data](https://irma.nps.gov/STATS/SSRSReports/National%20Reports/Current%20Year%20Monthly%20and%20Annual%20Summary%20Report%20(1979%20-%20Present))
obtained from the National Parks Service.

In comparison, Yosemite National Park experienced a drop of 50% when it
comes to visitation from almost 4.2 million visitors in 2019 to just
over 2 million in 2020. As the park opened up, members of the public
began to flood in.

Many campgrounds and facilities will still be
[closed](https://www.nps.gov/yose/planyourvisit/covid19.htm) because of
the Covid-19 pandemic as cases continue to fluctuate. Yosemite officials
even decided that some of the more frequented campgrounds will require
something other than reservations. Piloting a lottery system for the
campground, the limited access to the park that was established out of
necessity at the start of the pandemic may continue to function at
Yosemite. Whether this affects visitation is still to be determined as
Yosemite National Park continues to be an incredibly popular spot to
visit in California.
